## Why Bali

I'd been skeptical. Bali has a reputation for being overrun with digital nomads and Instagram-optimized retreats. But a friend's insistence and a cheap flight convinced me. I'm glad it did — you just have to know where to go.

Based in Canggu for the first week, Ubud for the last three days.

## Learning to Surf at Batu Bolong

Took a 3-day surf course with a local instructor named Made at Batu Bolong beach. Day one was humbling. The waves were gentle by any measure, and I still couldn't get upright without falling.

By day three something clicked. It's not about strength — it's about reading the wave before it reaches you, timing your pop-up, and keeping your weight centered. Still very much a beginner, but I caught enough waves to understand why people get obsessed.

## The Rice Terraces of Tegallalang

Rented a motorbike and rode up to Tegallalang in the early morning before the tour buses arrived. The terraces are genuinely extraordinary — the geometry of them, carved into the hillsides over centuries, is unlike anything I'd seen. Walked the trails for two hours without seeing another tourist.

## Ubud

Ubud feels like a different island from Canggu. Quieter, more focused on arts and culture. Visited the Monkey Forest, which is exactly as chaotic as you'd expect (a monkey stole my water bottle). The morning markets are worth an early alarm.

Found a small wayang kulit (shadow puppet) performance in a temple courtyard one evening — entirely by accident, entirely free — that was one of the best live performances I've seen anywhere.

## Notes for Next Time

- Stay further from central Canggu if you want peace
- Rent a motorbike — it's how you get to the good places
- The coffee is legitimately excellent everywhere
- Go to Nusa Penida if you have an extra day

Bali earned its reputation back. Just come with expectations calibrated appropriately and it delivers.
