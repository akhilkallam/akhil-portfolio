## Why a Marathon

I'd been running casually for two years — 3 or 4 miles a few times a week, the occasional 10K race. The jump to marathon seemed absurd. Then a colleague signed up and asked if I wanted to join. That's basically how it always happens.

We signed up for a race 16 weeks out. I downloaded a training plan and started the next Monday.

## The Training Plan

Used a modified version of Hal Higdon's Novice 1 plan. Four runs per week — three shorter weekday runs and one long run on Sunday that increased by roughly a mile each week.

**Week 1:** 3, 3, 3, 6 miles
**Week 8 (peak):** 5, 5, 5, 20 miles

The long runs are where you learn what you're made of.

## What Actually Happened

### Weeks 1–5: Deceptively Fine

The first month felt manageable. Long runs topped out around 10–12 miles. I was running slower than felt natural, which is correct — easy runs should be conversational pace.

### Weeks 6–10: The Hard Middle

This is where most people either build real fitness or fall apart. I came close to the latter. Hit a bad IT band flare around week 8 that had me questioning whether I'd make the start line.

Took four days off completely. Did hip strengthening exercises I found via a physical therapist's YouTube channel. Came back slower and more cautious. The IT band didn't fully clear until week 12.

### Weeks 11–16: Taper and Terror

The 20-mile long run in week 14 was the peak. Hardest physical thing I'd done. Took the next two weeks tapering — shorter runs, more rest — and spent most of that time convinced I was losing fitness (I wasn't).

## Race Day

The first 13 miles felt easy — too easy. I slowed down every time I noticed I was running faster than my target pace.

Mile 18 is where it stopped being easy. The classic wall. My legs didn't want to keep moving and I had to negotiate with them for every mile after that.

Crossed the finish in 4:22. Not fast. Felt incredible.

## What I'd Tell Past Me

- Run your easy runs easier than feels right
- Do the hip and glute strength work from the start, not after injury
- The taper is not losing fitness — trust it
- Mile 18 is going to hurt and that's normal

Would I do another one? I told myself no for about two weeks. Then I started looking at spring races.
